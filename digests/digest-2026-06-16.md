## 📄 Research Papers — Top Picks
- **SING: Synthetic Intention Graph for Scalable Active Tool Discovery in LLM Agents** — Xiao et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16591
  As agent tool ecosystems grow to thousands of APIs, injecting every tool schema becomes costly and assumes a closed inventory. SING builds an intention–tool graph linking user intentions, tool capabilities, and collaboration patterns, then retrieves tools dynamically as the task state evolves. On three real-world benchmarks over 7,471 tools it lifts Global Recall@5 by up to 59.8% and success rate by up to 28.9% while cutting full-corpus schema exposure by 99.8%. **Why it matters:** Makes large-scale tool-use practical by retrieving the right tools on demand instead of flooding context.

- **PACT: Privileged Trace Co-Training for Multi-Turn Tool-Use Agents** — Du et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16215
  Post-training multi-turn tool agents is hard: RL suffers sparse rewards while SFT on expert traces over-constrains the model. PACT uses expert traces only as a training-time signal — keeping rollouts prompt-only — via a trace-conditioned RL surrogate plus a component-aware SFT loss with annealed strength and prompt-only anchoring. On FTRL, BFCL, and ToolHop it consistently beats strong SFT and RL baselines. **Why it matters:** A cleaner recipe for teaching agents to call tools across turns without locking them into fixed trajectories.

- **PrologMCP: A Standardized Prolog Tool Interface for LLM Agents** — Mensfelt et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14935
  Reasoning-tuned LLMs still fail deep deductive tasks, and longer internal reasoning scales poorly. PrologMCP exposes Prolog as a stateful tool over the Model Context Protocol, giving MCP agents a reusable translate-run-inspect-repair loop with structured errors and per-session isolation. A formalizer agent matches or exceeds Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-4.1, and o4-mini on PARARULE-Plus, staying near-perfect where reasoning LLMs drop to ~0.94. **Why it matters:** Shows symbolic delegation via MCP is a robust, inspectable alternative to ever-longer chain-of-thought.

- **TechRAG: Evidence-Gated Multimodal Agentic RAG for Technical Literature Reasoning** — Singh · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01613
  TechRAG replaces single-pass RAG with an autonomous, evidence-gated pipeline over a multi-thousand-paper technical corpus. It rewrites text and visual queries separately, fuses FAISS+BM25 retrieval with cross-encoder reranking and graph traversal over a Neo4j knowledge graph, scores evidence sufficiency on a 100-point rubric, and generates cited answers through Planner/Researcher/Writer/Critic agents. **Why it matters:** A concrete blueprint for grounded, citation-verified RAG over specialized scientific literature.

- **SciOrch: Learning to Orchestrate Expert LLMs for Frontier Multimodal Scientific Reasoning** — Guo et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15872
  Different frontier models excel at different question types, so SciOrch trains a lightweight 8B orchestrator to decompose questions, delegate sub-problems to selected commercial models via API, and synthesize answers. Because each action is an expensive API call, it trains via MCTS rollouts plus GRPO-style optimization instead of online RL. It reaches 56.66% on a 240-question science set, beating the best single model by 3.74% at under half the API cost of typical multi-agent methods. **Why it matters:** Routing across complementary frontier models can beat any single one — at lower cost.

- **AdaSTORM: Scaling LLM Reasoning on Dynamic Graphs via Adaptive Spatio-Temporal Multi-Agent Collaboration** — Hao et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16328
  LLMs choke on dynamic-graph reasoning beyond tens of nodes due to exponential overhead and context limits. AdaSTORM adaptively partitions large dynamic graphs into model-sized subregions, then aligns those partitions with a spatio-temporal decoupled multi-agent architecture for collaborative reasoning. It scales to thousand-node graphs at over 90% accuracy without external tools, beating seven baselines. **Why it matters:** First multi-agent framework that breaks the graph-size ceiling for temporal reasoning tasks.

- **XFlow: An Executable Protocol Programming System for Reliable Multi-Agent Workflows** — Li et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14790
  Multi-agent reliability suffers from an underspecified prompt–harness boundary. XFlow and its XPF protocol language sit between prompt-only orchestration and rigid markup: protocols read literately but compile and execute as programs, moving selected commitments into checkable harness structure while keeping semantic work inside actors. Lifecycle-governed typed state cells mediate actor outputs before they become shared state. **Why it matters:** Offers a principled way to decide what belongs in prompts vs. enforced workflow structure.

- **Nemotron 3 Ultra: Open MoE Hybrid Mamba-Transformer for Agentic Reasoning** — NVIDIA · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15007
  NVIDIA releases Nemotron 3 Ultra, a 550B-total / 55B-active MoE hybrid Mamba-Attention model pretrained on 20T tokens, extended to a 1M-token context, and post-trained with SFT, RL, and multi-teacher on-policy distillation. It delivers up to ~6x higher inference throughput than comparable LLMs at on-par accuracy, with base, post-trained, and quantized checkpoints plus data and recipe open-sourced. **Why it matters:** An open, high-throughput long-context model purpose-built for long-running autonomous agents.

- **Are Online Skill and Memory Modules Always Worth Their Tokens?** — Hajimiri et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15017
  Web agents often bolt on memory, workflow, or skill modules, but rarely account for the test-time tokens these consume. Under a fixed inference budget, a token-matched vanilla baseline matches or beats AWM, ASI, and ReasoningBank across three WebArena domains and three models — often using fewer tokens. The authors also show run-to-run variance materially affects outcomes. **Why it matters:** A sobering, budget-aware reminder that fancy agent memory often doesn't beat just spending the tokens on more actor steps.

- **Not All Skills Help: Measuring and Repairing Agent Knowledge** — Wang et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15390
  Self-improving agents accumulate natural-language skills, but skill libraries show pervasive causal heterogeneity — a skill helps some tasks while hurting others, cancelling out in aggregate. ASSAY separates generation from curation: it computes per-skill causal attribution on a small dev set, restructures the library offline, and suppresses skills with negative predicted effect per task. On AppWorld's hardest split DeepSeek-V3 hits 69.3% (47.4% relative gain), beating weight-tuned methods. **Why it matters:** The bottleneck is matching skills to tasks at inference, not pruning globally — a shift in how agent memory should be curated.

- **From Argument Components to Graphs: Multi-Agent Debate with Confidence Gating** — Bąba & Chudziak · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16047
  Training-free LLMs miss subtle argument relations and self-correction can reinforce hallucinations. This work extends a Proponent-Opponent-Judge debate to argument relation identification, adding confidence gating so agents debate only uncertain cases and accept confident initial predictions. On UKP Argument Annotated Essays v2 the selective debate gets the highest Macro F1 among training-free methods and beats fine-tuned RoBERTa, while debating everything degrades results. **Why it matters:** Gating debate to uncertain cases is both cheaper and more accurate than debating all of them.

- **Towards Pareto-Optimal Tool-Integrated Agents with Pareto Ranking Policy Optimization** — Li et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16111
  Most tool-agent alignment maximizes accuracy alone, ignoring tool-use efficiency. ParetoPO is a two-stage multi-objective framework: hypervolume-guided dynamic scalarization adapts reward weights to Pareto-frontier progress, then Pareto-ranking-based advantage computation promotes nondominated trajectories via dominance-aware credit assignment. On math reasoning and multi-hop QA it finds better accuracy–efficiency trade-offs than static baselines. **Why it matters:** Trains agents to balance accuracy against tool-call cost rather than optimizing accuracy in a vacuum.

- **Benchmarking LLM Agents on Meta-Analysis Articles from Nature Portfolio** — Xie et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17041
  MetaSyn offers 442 expert-curated meta-analyses from Nature journals, each pairing a question with PI/ECO criteria, a 140k-article PubMed corpus, verified inclusions, and hard negatives. Benchmarking twelve pipelines exposes a screening bottleneck: despite 90.9% retrieval recall at K=200, no system recovers more than 52.7% of ground-truth included studies. **Why it matters:** Pinpoints study screening — not retrieval — as where evidence-synthesis agents break, with stage-attributed metrics to diagnose it.

- **S1-DeepResearch: Beyond Search, Toward Real-World Long-Horizon Research Agents** — Dong et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15367
  Most deep-research training data is search-centric, training information-seeking but neglecting evidence integration, planning, file understanding, and report writing. S1-DeepResearch builds a unified trajectory paradigm combining closed-ended QA with open-ended exploration via graph-grounded task formulation and multi-dimensional verification. S1-DeepResearch-32B reaches state-of-the-art among comparable open models across 20 benchmarks and approaches frontier proprietary models on hard tasks. **Why it matters:** Jointly modeling acquisition, synthesis, and planning yields stronger research agents than search-only training.

- **Beyond NL2Code: A Structured Survey of Multimodal Code Intelligence** — Zhao et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15932
  Many real programming tasks specify intent through screenshots, charts, documents, vector drawings, and interactive states, requiring models to connect visual perception to executable programs. This survey organizes the field by code's role (rendered artifact, editable structure, scientific representation, reasoning trace, or tool interface) across GUI, scientific visualization, structured graphics, and frontier agentic settings, and argues for verification-centered directions. **Why it matters:** A map of multimodal code generation that pushes the field from single-output imitation toward evidence-grounded, executable systems.

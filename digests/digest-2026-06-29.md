## 📄 Research Papers — Top Picks
- **ProMSA: Progressive Multimodal Search Agents for Knowledge-Based Visual Question Answering** — ZhengXian Wu et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27974
  ProMSA is a multimodal search agent for KB-VQA that iteratively chooses image search, text search, or stop under explicit tool-call budgets with deduplication, replacing the fixed retrieve-then-generate pipeline. It is trained via rejection-sampling SFT for valid tool-use formats, then optimized with TN-GSPO, a sequence-level RL objective normalized by generation length and tool-interaction depth. Gains over strong RAG and agent baselines on E-VQA and InfoSeek. **Why it matters:** Shows adaptive, budget-aware tool-calling beats static top-k retrieval for knowledge-grounded multimodal reasoning.

- **Just Ask: Curious Code Agents Reveal System Prompts in Frontier LLMs** — Xiang Zheng et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21233
  JustAsk is a self-evolving framework that autonomously discovers system-prompt extraction strategies through standard interaction alone, using UCB-based strategy selection over a hierarchical skill space of atomic probes and orchestration. Across 41 black-box commercial models it achieves full or near-complete system-prompt recovery, with no handcrafted prompts or privileged access. **Why it matters:** Exposes system prompts as a critical, largely unprotected attack surface that agentic autonomy systematically widens.

- **Dialogue to Detection: A Multimodal Hybrid NLP Pipeline for Insurance Fraud Detection** — Muhammad Shakeel Akram et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28002
  A synthetic multimodal framework replicates first-notice-of-loss conditions, generating agent-customer dialogue transcripts and two-speaker audio, then running ASR and diarisation. Downstream it combines NER, regex feature extraction, LLM-RAG retrieval, and speaker embeddings into a rule-based risk score flagging narrative reuse, structural inconsistencies, and cross-case voice repetition. **Why it matters:** Offers a reproducible multimodal baseline beyond text-only fraud detection, useful where private datasets block progress.

- **Hybrid Fact-Checking that Integrates Knowledge Graphs, LLMs, and Search-Based Retrieval Agents** — Shaghayegh Kolli et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03217
  This pipeline chains a fast one-hop DBpedia KG lookup, an LM classifier with rule-based logic, and a web search agent invoked only when KG coverage is insufficient. It hits F1 0.93 on FEVER Supported/Refuted without task-specific fine-tuning, and reannotation shows it frequently recovers valid evidence for claims originally labeled Not Enough Information. **Why it matters:** A modular, interpretable fallback-driven design that gets strong claim verification without fine-tuning.

- **Learning to Evict from Key-Value Cache** — Luca Moschella, Laura Manduchi, Ozan Sener · https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10238
  KV Policy (KVP) reframes KV-cache eviction as RL: lightweight per-head agents trained on pre-computed generation traces rank tokens by predicted future utility, requiring no model changes or extra inference cost. On RULER (up to 128K) and OASST2-4k it beats strong heuristic baselines and generalizes zero-shot to longer sequences and downstream tasks. **Why it matters:** Learned token-utility prediction is a scalable path to cheaper long-context inference versus recency/attention heuristics.

- **LLawCo: Learning Laws of Cooperation for Modeling Embodied Multi-Agent Behavior** — Qinhong Zhou, Chuang Gan, Anoop Cherian · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28182
  LLawCo lets embodied agents reflect on past failures to extract misaligned patterns and derive high-level behavioral laws (e.g. "Talk when necessary," "Wait for partner"), incorporated into chains of thought via SFT. On the new PARTNR-Dialog benchmark and TDW-MAT it improves success rates by 4.5% and 6.8% across four backbone LLMs. **Why it matters:** Turns failure reflection into reusable, explicit cooperation rules that improve decentralized multi-agent coordination.

- **AgentPSO: Evolving Agent Reasoning Skill via Multi-agent Particle Swarm Optimization** — Hyunmin Hwang et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08704
  AgentPSO treats each agent as a particle whose state is a natural-language skill and whose velocity is a semantic update direction, combining personal-best, global-best, and self-reflective directions to evolve reasoning skills without touching backbone parameters. Evolved skills improve over static single-agent and test-time-only baselines and transfer across benchmarks and models. **Why it matters:** Demonstrates training-free skill evolution that captures reusable reasoning procedures rather than prompt overfitting.

- **Hierarchical Control in Multi-Agent Games: LLM-based Planning and RL Execution** — Jannik Hösch et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20014
  A pretrained LLM acts as a centralized strategic controller selecting among specialized RL skill policies, while RL handles reactive low-level execution, evaluated in a competitive 2v2 King of the Hill game. The LLM+RL system matches hand-crafted behavior trees statistically and beats flat end-to-end RL, with 60% of users finding it the most human-like. **Why it matters:** Shows LLM reasoning can orchestrate pretrained RL skills for competitive coordination without manual rule engineering.

- **Yuvion LLM: An Adversarially-Aware Large Language Model for Content And AI Safety** — Ting Ma et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27632
  Yuvion treats adversarial robustness and agentic capability as first-class objectives, combining adversarially-aware data, knowledge-enhanced continued pretraining, and policy-grounded multi-task safety post-training including safety-aware agentic RL for tool use. It introduces RiskEval (93 benchmarks) and reports Yuvion-8B outperforming much larger models on several safety tasks under adversarial conditions. **Why it matters:** Frames safety as inherently adversarial and shows small models can lead on robustness when trained for it directly.

- **When Search Agents Should Ask: DiscoBench for Clarification-Aware Deep Search** — Yiling Tao et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27669
  DiscoBench evaluates whether search agents can proactively detect ambiguity, ask effective clarification questions, and recover correct reasoning paths, with 211 samples and 463 ambiguity instances across 11 domains plus a user simulator. Experiments show ambiguity detection and clarification are distinct skills, and repeatedly searching instead of asking often underperforms direct guessing. **Why it matters:** Highlights interactive clarification as a missing capability that retrieval-strong deep-search agents still lack.

- **Mitigating Position Bias in Transformers via Layer-Specific Positional Embedding Scaling** — Changze Lv et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27705
  LPES assigns distinct RoPE scaling factors per layer to balance attention without fine-tuning or added latency, using a genetic algorithm with Bézier curves to shrink the search space. It mitigates the lost-in-the-middle problem with up to 11.2% accuracy gain on key-value retrieval. **Why it matters:** A cheap, training-free tweak to long-context attention that directly attacks middle-context information loss.

- **Verifiable Geometry Problem Solving: Solver-Driven Autoformalization and Theorem Proposing** — Can Li et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27926
  SD-GPS makes the symbolic solver an execution oracle across both autoformalization (solvability-guided RL on QwenVL3-2B) and deduction, where an impasse-aware agent proposes auxiliary lemmas filtered through symbolic verification. It outperforms MLLM, neural, and neuro-symbolic methods on Geometry3K and PGPS9K. **Why it matters:** Closing the loop between multimodal perception and symbolic execution yields verifiable, soundness-guaranteed reasoning.

- **Lost at the End: Primacy Bias in Multimodal Retrieval-Augmented Question Answering** — Jieyuan Liu et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16494
  A controlled gold-position probe across three 7B/8B VLM readers and two KB-VQA benchmarks finds the classic U-shaped lost-in-the-middle effect flips to strong primacy, with gold-at-first beating gold-at-last by 16–26 points. Three retrieval-side fixes leave the gap intact, pinning the cause to the reader's prompt slot 0. **Why it matters:** Shows recall@k is the wrong metric for deployed multimodal RAG and that reader-side intervention is required.

- **EXPLORE-Bench: Egocentric Scene Prediction with Long-Horizon Reasoning** — Chengjun Yu et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09731
  EXPLORE-Bench, curated from real first-person videos, asks models to predict the final scene after a sequence of atomic actions, with structured annotations of object categories, attributes, and relations. Experiments reveal a large human-MLLM gap; stepwise decomposition helps somewhat but adds non-trivial compute. **Why it matters:** Provides a principled testbed exposing how poorly current MLLMs reason about long-horizon physical consequences for embodied agents.

- **The Strongest Teacher Is Not Always the Best Teacher: Student-Centric Answer Selection** — Zhengyu Hu et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26872
  SCAS selects among verified teacher-generated answers by estimated student-centric learning cost, derived from a token-wise gradient decomposition into an efficient forward-only proxy. Across 30 teachers, 6 students, and 6 tasks it consistently beats picking the strongest teacher. **Why it matters:** Reframes distillation around supervision matched to the current student rather than raw teacher strength.

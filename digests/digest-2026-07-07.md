## 📄 Research Papers — Top Picks

- **Unified Audio Intelligence Without Regressing on Text Intelligence** — NVIDIA/Nemotron team · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05196
  Introduces Audex, a unified audio-text LLM built on a strong text-only MoE backbone (Nemotron-Cascade-2-30B-A3B), using a single decoder where audio is projected into text embedding space. Trained on 157B audio + 320B text tokens with multi-stage SFT, RL, and distillation, it achieves SOTA audio understanding/generation while preserving the base model's reasoning and agentic capabilities. **Why it matters:** demonstrates audio-native LLMs no longer need to trade off text reasoning quality.

- **Nemotron-Labs-3-Puzzle-75B-A9B: Compressing Hybrid MoE LLMs** — NVIDIA/Nemotron team · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04371
  Presents a compressed variant of Nemotron-3-Super using iterative pruning, distillation, RL, quantization and Multi-Token Prediction, achieving ~2x server throughput and 8x long-context concurrency on a single H100 vs. the parent model. Retains strong accuracy across reasoning, coding, and agentic benchmarks despite heavy compression. **Why it matters:** practical recipe for deploying large hybrid MoE models efficiently without major quality loss.

- **MedBench v5: A Dynamic, Process-Oriented, and Hallucination-Aware Benchmark for Clinical Multimodal Models** — Ding et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24155
  Redesigns clinical AI evaluation around process auditing rather than static QA, adding stressors (omission, contradiction, evidence delay) and hallucination-propagation tracking across reasoning steps for language/vision-language/agent systems. Finds that strong overall scores can mask unstable reasoning under stress. **Why it matters:** raises the bar for how clinical AI systems should be validated before deployment.

- **Artificial Intelligence for Mathematical Reasoning: An Integrated Survey** — Raiyan et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08728
  Comprehensive survey spanning informal (MWP/geometry/VLM) to formal (proof assistants, autoformalization) mathematical reasoning, covering CoT, tool use, process reward models, and RLVR, plus a critical look at benchmark saturation and contamination. **Why it matters:** a reference map for anyone building or evaluating math-reasoning LLMs.

- **LLMoxie: Exploring Agentic AI for Scientific Software Development** — Setiawan et al. (UW) · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02703
  Describes a 20-month deployment of an institutional AI platform with a Plugin-Agent-Skill hierarchy for research software engineering, adapting off-the-shelf coding agents to scientific conventions (reproducibility, provenance, sensitive-data handling). **Why it matters:** concrete lessons on adapting generic coding agents for regulated/scientific domains.

- **Agentic and Generative AI for OSINT and Cyber Investigations: Taxonomy, Evaluation, Challenges** — Palmieri et al. · https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03233
  Systematic review of 74 studies on LLM/agentic AI for OSINT, proposing an 11-category taxonomy and identifying a "hallucination-validation gap" — hallucination is widely flagged as a risk but rarely measured end-to-end in OSINT settings. **Why it matters:** highlights a concrete evaluation blind spot before agentic OSINT tools are trusted operationally.
